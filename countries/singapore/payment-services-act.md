# Payment Services Act

## Executive Summary
The Payment Services Act (PS Act) provides a comprehensive regulatory framework for payment systems and payment service providers in Singapore. It regulates seven types of payment services, ensuring consumer protection and promoting innovation in the digital payment space.

## Background
Introduced to replace the older Payment Systems (Oversight) Act and Money-changing and Remittance Businesses Act, the PS Act was designed to future-proof Singapore's payment regulations, specifically addressing the rise of digital payment tokens (crypto) and e-money.

## Scope
The Act covers seven payment service types:
1. Account issuance
2. Domestic money transfer
3. Cross-border money transfer
4. Merchant acquisition
5. E-money issuance
6. Digital payment token (DPT) service
7. Money-changing

## Key Requirements
- **Licensing:** Entities must apply for a Standard, Major, or Money-Changing license based on transaction volume.
- **Base Capital:** Minimum base capital requirements ranging from SGD 100,000 to SGD 1,000,000 depending on the license type.
- **Safeguarding:** Customer money and e-money must be safeguarded in trust accounts or via guarantees.

## Compliance Obligations
- Implement robust Anti-Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) programs.
- Conduct regular independent audits.
- Submit periodic regulatory returns to MAS.

## Affected Institutions
- Banks, Electronic Money Institutions (EMIs), Remittance Agents, Crypto Exchanges, and Wallet Providers.

## Affected Payment Systems
- FAST, PayNow, PayNow Corporate, MEPS+.

## ISO 20022 Impact
MAS has mandated the migration of all domestic payment systems (including FAST and MEPS+) to the ISO 20022 standard, requiring licensed institutions to upgrade their messaging formats.

## API Impact
While the PS Act itself does not mandate Open Banking APIs, MAS encourages API-driven innovation through its API registers and the Open API initiative.

## Operational Impact
Institutions must upgrade their transaction monitoring systems, update customer onboarding (KYC) flows, and ensure strict segregation of customer funds in their accounting systems.

## Reporting Requirements
- Annual audited financial statements.
- Quarterly transaction volume and value reports.
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) to STRO.

## Compliance Timeline
- Full licensing required by January 28, 2020 (for existing entities).
- Ongoing compliance with updated AML/CFT notices.

## Exceptions
- Certain entities regulated under the Banking Act or Securities and Futures Act may be exempted from specific PS Act licensing requirements for overlapping activities.
